STORIES

Following a failed attempt in 2014, Paul Valin embarked on a second expedition to Mount Everest in 2018, after being motivated by his team member Henry's final year of climbing before retirement...

The entire expedition took five weeks to complete. The most grueling part of the climb was the final push to the summit from Camp Four, which began at 8 p.m. after 15 hours of no sleep. After seven hours of climbing, the team reached a "balcony" at 3 a.m., where the climber experienced a perilous drop in body temperature, leaving him unable to feel his fingers. This extremely unsafe situation was overcome by finding a newfound mental strength, which, along with the rising sun, gave him the determination to continue. At 7:10 a.m. on May 21st, he reached the summit where he spent 40 minutes before beginning the treacherous 12-hour descent.

Reflecting on the climb, he believes his success was 50% luck and 50% a combination of mental strength, physical ability, and technical skills, with mental fortitude being the most critical factor. The experience taught him about his limits and the importance of teamwork.
