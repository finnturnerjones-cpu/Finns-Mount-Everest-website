LEARN THE ROUTE

Base Camps: The journey begins at the Chinese Base Camp 18,300 ft, which is accessible by road. Climbers then trek to the Advanced Base Camp (ABC) at 21,300 ft.

North Col: From ABC, climbers ascend the East Rongbuk Glacier to reach the North Col (Camp 1) at around 23,100 ft. This section involves fixed ropes on a steep, 60-degree snow and ice slope.

High Camps: The route continues up the North Ridge to a series of high-altitude camps. Camp 2 is typically placed at 25,600 ft, and Camp 3 at 27,200 ft.

The Three Steps: The most technically challenging sections are the three rocky outcrops, known as the First, Second, and Third Steps, located at extreme altitude on the Northeast Ridge.

First Step 28,100 ft: A section of large, exposed boulders that is difficult to climb.

Second Step 28,250 ft: The crux of the climb, a near-vertical 30-foot rock wall. Climbers use a ladder to ascend this section.

Third Step 28,600 ft: A smaller but still difficult rock climb just below the summit pyramid.
