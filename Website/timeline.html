TIMELINE

1921: Reconnaissance expedition

a reconnaissance mission from the north side to map the region and find a potential route to the summit.

1922: First attempt

The second British expedition to Mount Everest in 1922 set a new world altitude record of 8,321 meters with the use of supplemental oxygen, suggesting a potential route to the summit was achievable.

1924: Mallory and Irvine disappearance

The 1924 British expedition to Mount Everest saw Edward Norton set a new altitude record and involved the disappearance of George Mallory and Andrew Irvine near the summit, leading to long-standing debate about whether they successfully reached the top before their deaths.

1933: Failure at the Second Step

The 1933 British expedition to Mount Everest failed despite using a high assault camp, as climbers following Norton's 1924 route decided the Second Step was "unclimbable" and were forced to turn back due to poor conditions and altitude-related issues.

1951: Defeat at the Khumbu Icefall

led by Eric Shipton and including Edmund Hillary, surveyed a potential new southern route through Nepal but was ultimately defeated by impassable crevasses in the Khumbu Icefall.

1953: The First Ascent

The ninth British expedition to Mount Everest, led by John Hunt, culminated in the historic first documented ascent of the summit on May 29, 1953, by Edmund Hillary and Tenzing Norgay, after a first assault team had successfully reached the South Summit.

1963: The first American to climb

In 1963, Jim Whittaker became the first American to climb Everest, and later that month, Tom Hornbein and Willi Unsoeld completed the first traverse of the mountain via a new route up the Hornbein Couloir.

1970: Skiing down everest

In 1970, Japanese adventurer Yuichiro Miura became the first person to ski from the South Col of Everest.

1975: First Woman to reach the summit

On May 16, 1975, Junko Tabei of Japan became the first woman to reach the summit of Mount Everest, overcoming an avalanche during her ascent, and she later became the first woman to complete the Seven Summits in 1992.

1984: Second summit from the west ridge

Bulgarian climber Hristo Prodanov became the first from his country to summit Everest by the West Ridge but died on the descent, while a second Bulgarian team completed the second-ever traverse of the mountain by ascending the West Ridge and descending the South Col.

1996: Mount Everest deadliest year

In 1996, the deadliest year in Mount Everest's history, fifteen people died, including climbers from two major expeditions trapped by a severe storm.

2014: Disaster on Everest

A massive ice avalanche in the Khumbu Icefall killed 16 Nepalese guides, leading to a Sherpa strike that effectively shut down the climbing season on the Nepal side and brought global attention to the dangerous working conditions of local workers.

2019: Mount Everest Becomes Overcrowded

Record numbers of climbers led to extreme overcrowding and "traffic jams" in the "death zone," resulting in 11 deaths and widespread media coverage that sparked major debates about the commercialization, regulation, and ethics of climbing Everest.
