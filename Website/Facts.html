FACTS

Geology:
Height:
Mount everest is 29,031.7 feet (8,848.86 meters) tall.

Mounting Growing:
The mountain grows about 0.25 inches (4 millimeters) each year as the Indian tectonic plate continues to push against the Asian plate.

Composition:
Mount Everest is made of different types of rocks, including shale, limestone, and marble. 

Location and naming:
Location:
It is situated on the border between Nepal (south) and China (Tibet, north).

Local names:
It is called Chomolungma in Tibetan, meaning "Mother Goddess of the World," and Sagarmatha in Nepali, meaning "Goddess of the Sky".

English name:
It was named after Sir George Everest, a former Surveyor General of India.

Climbing and conditions
 Duration:
It takes 40 to 60 days for a climber to attempt the summit, allowing their body to acclimatize to the high altitude.

Death Zone:
The area above 24,900 feet (7,600 meters) is known as the "Death Zone" due to its extremely thin air, where survival is difficult and requires supplemental oxygen.

Dangers:
Climbers face risks from avalanches, blizzards, and difficult terrain.

First successful ascent
The first successful climb was in 1953 by Sir Edmund Hillary of New Zealand and Tenzing Norgay, a Nepalese Sherpa guide.

Sherpas:
The Sherpa people are known for their exceptional strength and experience in mountain climbing at high altitudes, often serving as guides.
